WORK

I love coding, working with people and learning new technology.

I've worked in the tech industry since 2006

My first tech job was at Kamahi Electronics where I worked for 7 years. I was involved with many commercially successful product developments. I built, tested and fixed high end scientific equipment. Designed multi-layer PCBs for complex mixed signal equipment. I used intuition, tenacity and knowledge from mulitple domains to diagnose and fix difficult technical issues. I learned embedded C programming for microcontrollers and my code is running in the wild on scientific, sporting and safety products.

While working at Kamahi Electronics my brother and I started a contract electronics manufacturing business to offload Kamahi's production. The business ran for 2 years in which time we produced several hundred research quality scientific instruments for export to The Netherlands. We had a few students working for us on casual contracts and learned a lot. Eventually it got too much as I was still working for Kamahi at the same time and we decided to close down.

After leaving Kamahi and moving to Wellington I worked as a freelance electronics and embedded developer, often contracting back to Kamahi or Hunter Safety Lab among others.

Most recently I worked as a facilitating teacher for Enspiral Dev Academy. I taught or helped teach more than 100 developers to code in my time there. I facilitated students through difficult conversations, group conflict, family crises, emotional breakdowns and general bootcamp fatigue. My job required that I role modeled vulnerability and empathetic listening just as much as competently live coding problems in front of a classroom. I love working with teams of people, every team is different and has its own unique character.

I'm a full-stack web developer for hire, and member of the Protozoa cooperative.
